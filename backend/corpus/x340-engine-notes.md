# X-340 Small Engine — Service Notes

The X-340 is a fictional single-cylinder, air-cooled 340 cc engine used here as a
demonstration source. These notes describe routine maintenance for a typical
horizontal-shaft installation. All figures below are invented for the demo and
should not be applied to any real machine.

## Specifications at a glance

- Displacement: 340 cc, single cylinder, overhead valve
- Cooling: forced air, flywheel-driven fan shroud
- Lubrication: splash system, 1.1 L crankcase capacity
- Recommended oil: SAE 10W-30 above 0 °C; SAE 5W-30 below freezing
- Spark plug gap: 0.75 mm
- Fuel: unleaded, 87 octane minimum, no more than 10% ethanol

## Torque specifications

Fasteners on the X-340 are torqued in two stages — finger-tight, then to final
value in a crossing pattern. The cylinder-head bolts take a final torque of
**22 Nm**, applied in three passes (10 Nm, 16 Nm, then 22 Nm) following a
diagonal sequence to seat the head gasket evenly. The flywheel nut is torqued to
75 Nm, and the connecting-rod cap bolts to 12 Nm. Over-torquing the head bolts
is the most common cause of gasket failure on this engine, so a calibrated
torque wrench is required rather than a breaker bar.

## Oil change interval

Change the oil after the first 5 hours of break-in, then every 50 operating
hours or once per season, whichever comes first. Warm the engine for two minutes
before draining so the oil carries away more suspended particles. Dispose of
used oil at an approved recycling point.

## Air filter

The X-340 uses a dual-element filter: a foam pre-cleaner wrapping a pleated
paper cartridge. Tap the paper element clean every 25 hours and replace it every
100 hours. Wash the foam pre-cleaner in warm soapy water, dry it fully, and work
a few drops of clean engine oil through it before reinstalling. Never oil the
paper element.

## Storage

For storage beyond 30 days, either add fuel stabilizer and run the engine for
ten minutes to circulate it, or drain the fuel system completely. Draining is
preferred in humid climates. Pull the starter slowly until resistance is felt so
both valves close, which keeps moisture out of the cylinder.

## Common faults

A hard-starting X-340 is usually starved of fuel from a gummed carburetor jet
after long storage, not an ignition fault. If the engine surges at idle, suspect
a lean condition from a partially blocked pilot jet before adjusting the
governor.
