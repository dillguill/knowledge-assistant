# Home Backup Battery Comparison

This note compares three fictional home backup batteries to demonstrate how the
assistant answers from uploaded sources. The products, prices, and figures below
are invented for the demo and describe no real hardware.

## What a backup battery does

A home backup battery stores electricity — usually from the grid at off-peak
hours or from rooftop solar — and releases it during an outage or when grid
prices spike. Two numbers matter most: usable **capacity** (kWh), which sets how
long it runs your loads, and continuous **power output** (kW), which sets how
many appliances it can run at once.

## The three units

### AuroraCell Home 10

- Usable capacity: 10.0 kWh
- Continuous output: 5 kW (7 kW peak for 10 seconds)
- Chemistry: lithium iron phosphate (LFP)
- Round-trip efficiency: 90%
- Warranty: 10 years or 6,000 cycles
- Indicative price: $7,200 installed

The AuroraCell is the balanced choice: enough power to run a well pump or a
window air-conditioner, and LFP chemistry that tolerates daily cycling.

### VoltVault Slim 6

- Usable capacity: 6.4 kWh
- Continuous output: 3.3 kW
- Chemistry: LFP
- Round-trip efficiency: 89%
- Warranty: 10 years or 6,000 cycles
- Indicative price: $4,900 installed

The Slim 6 is the budget, small-footprint option. It keeps a fridge, lights, and
a router alive through an outage, but it cannot start large motor loads. Two
units can be wired in parallel to double both capacity and output.

### TitanStore Max 16

- Usable capacity: 16.0 kWh
- Continuous output: 9 kW
- Chemistry: nickel manganese cobalt (NMC)
- Round-trip efficiency: 92%
- Warranty: 10 years or 4,000 cycles
- Indicative price: $11,500 installed

The Max 16 is for whole-home backup including central air conditioning. Its NMC
chemistry is more energy-dense but has a lower cycle life, so it suits homes that
discharge deeply only occasionally rather than every day.

## Choosing between them

Size capacity to the hours of runtime you need and power to the largest load you
must start. For essential loads only — refrigerator, lights, phones, internet —
the VoltVault Slim 6 is usually enough. For comfort loads such as a heat pump,
step up to the AuroraCell Home 10. Choose the TitanStore Max 16 only when you
intend to ride through long outages with air conditioning running, and accept
the shorter cycle warranty in exchange for the extra headroom.

All three support grid-charging and solar-charging, and all three can island
your home automatically within a fraction of a second of an outage.
